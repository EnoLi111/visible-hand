Building a fairer future for women in precarious work.

Exploring the gendered impacts of insecure employment through research, policy analysis, and intervention design.

Why does precarious work affect women differently?

Women in unstable employment often experience increased emotional pressure, economic insecurity, and social expectations around caregiving. Our project investigates how gender inequality becomes embedded within modern labor structures and what interventions can support safer, more equitable workplaces.

Our Process

1 Research

We examined academic studies, policy reports, and feminist research surrounding precarious labor, workplace inequality, and emotional burden among women workers.

2 Analysis

Through thematic analysis, we identified recurring issues including unstable income, unpaid care work, workplace pressure, and mental health impacts on women in insecure employment.

3 Intervention

Our intervention uses digital storytelling and visual communication to raise awareness of gender inequality in precarious work while encouraging policy reflection and public discussion.
